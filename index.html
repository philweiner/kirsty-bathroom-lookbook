Lighting Decision Needed

Vanity Wall Light (Wall C, above sink)

Two approaches: G9 fixture + smart switch, or E27 fixture + smart colour-changing bulb. All controlled via FLIC button/Twist.

Two Smart Lighting Approaches

ApproachHow it worksPros & cons
🔵 Smart Switch
G9 fixture + Shelly/SONOFF		 Wire a tiny WiFi relay (Shelly 1 Mini or SONOFF MINIR4M) behind the light fitting. The relay sits on WiFi and responds to FLIC button presses via HTTP. The Ellis & Co pull cord stays in the circuit as a manual backup/isolation point — leave it "on" permanently. + Wider choice of IP44 bathroom fixtures
+ Cheaper overall
+ Simpler wiring
− No colour changing
− On/off only (dimming possible with Shelly Dimmer but tricky at low G9 wattage)
🟠 Smart Bulb
E27 fixture + LIFX Mini Color		 Choose an E27 fixture and leave it permanently powered on. A LIFX Mini Color smart bulb goes inside — FLIC button/Twist controls on/off, brightness, and colour via WiFi. The smart bulb draws ~0.5W in standby when "off". Pull cord stays in circuit for isolation. + Full RGB colour changing
+ Dimming built in
+ Warm orange glow or any mood colour on demand
− IP44 E27 fixtures are rarer/pricier
− LIFX bulb adds ~£30–40 to cost

IP44 required: The vanity light is in Zone 2 (within 600mm of the basin), so the fixture must be IP44 rated. Options marked IP20 can only be used if positioned more than 600mm from the basin edge.

Wall B Design Change

The non-shower section of Wall B (behind the toilet) now has Dormido wood effect tiles from floor to 1200 mm, with a light desert-style paint from 1200 mm to ceiling. The fluted green tiles have been removed from this wall. This creates a warm, cohesive look with the shower tiles while keeping the ceiling area light and airy.

Wall C Design Change

Wall C (window wall) now has fluted green tiles only up to ~1200 mm — a full-width band at the bottom (1828 × 897 mm) plus two small patches flanking the window sill (374 × 303 mm each). Everything above 1200 mm is plaster/drywall with paint, including around and above the window. This lines up with the paint on Wall B above the toilet, creating a consistent horizon line. Window reveals (127 mm each side) are also painted. Matteo tile area reduced from 6.643 m² to 4.534 m² (4.99 m² with wastage). 8 boxes already ordered — there will be spare tiles.
