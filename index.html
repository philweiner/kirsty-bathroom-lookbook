Coverage: 5.22 m² with 10% wastage (Wall C: 2.23 m² incl. reveals + sill | Wall D: 2.67 m²).

Interior Designer Notes

The palette in this room layers warm oak wood grain, cool grey terrazzo, brushed copper, matt black hardware, and desert-toned paint above the tile line. The replacement wall tile needs to bridge the warm and cool halves — something that picks up the copper and the terrazzo simultaneously without competing with the Dormido wood.

A blush or soft pink gloss is the right call here. It reads warm enough to sit next to the wood and copper, but has enough cool undertone to relate to the grey terrazzo floor. The glossy surface bounces light around what is a fairly compact room (1.8 × 2.1m), and a square format tiles cleanly around the window reveals without awkward cuts.

Of the options below, the Zellige Mini Pink (Walls & Floors) is the designer pick — the tonal variation in each tile gives it depth and character that a flat-colour tile can't match, and at 100×100mm it handles the 127mm window reveals and mirror cutouts on Wall D gracefully. The Wickes Flora via eBay is the best-value route if budget is a factor — same rustic handmade look at nearly half the retail price.

Wall B Design Change

The non-shower section of Wall B (behind the toilet) now has Dormido wood effect tiles from floor to 1200 mm, with a light desert-style paint from 1200 mm to ceiling. The fluted green tiles have been removed from this wall. This creates a warm, cohesive look with the shower tiles while keeping the ceiling area light and airy.

Wall C Design Change

Wall C (window wall) now has fluted green tiles only up to ~1200 mm — a full-width band at the bottom (1828 × 897 mm) plus two small patches flanking the window sill (374 × 303 mm each). Everything above 1200 mm is plaster/drywall with paint, including around and above the window. This lines up with the paint on Wall B above the toilet, creating a consistent horizon line.
