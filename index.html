Vertical (bottom)7 full rows (150mm) from mirror edge down + 1 cut row (46mm / 31%) at floor
Top strip177mm zone — see note below
Side strips1 column each side — 139mm wide (cut from 300mm tile)

Cuts hidden by the door: All horizontal cut tiles are on the RIGHT side (nearest to the bathroom door on Wall A). The bottom cut row at floor level is partially concealed by the radiator.
Top strip note: The top strip above the mirror is 177mm tall — taller than one landscape tile (150mm). Options: (1) One full 150mm row + 3mm grout + a narrow 24mm strip; (2) A single tile cut to 177mm from the 300mm dimension; (3) Adjust grout spacing in this zone. Discuss with tiler.
Flute alignment — CRITICAL: Stacked bond ensures flutes align row-to-row on full tiles. For the cut column on the right: ALWAYS cut from the RIGHT edge so the left-side flute pattern is preserved identically in every row. Same rule for side strips: always cut from the same edge. The floor cut row just shortens flutes (height trim) — no alignment issue. Wet saw: cut glazed face up for clean flute edges.

Gang 2 switches the KABOMBA vanity wall light. Gang 3 controls the mirror demister heater / extractor.
Electrician note — extractor wiring: The Airflow iCON 15 has a permanent live (L) + trigger (T) input. If wired with permanent live via an FCU, the fan can auto-sense humidity and run its overrun timer independently. Gang 3 could then control just the mirror demister. Discuss with electrician whether to wire extractor through the switch (simple, loses auto features) or on permanent live (keeps humidistat + overrun).
