Cuts hidden by the door: All horizontal cut tiles are on the RIGHT side of the wall (nearest to the bathroom door on Wall A). When the door is open, these cuts are the least visible. The bottom cut row at floor level is partially concealed by the radiator.
Top strip note: The top strip above the mirror is 177mm tall, which is taller than one landscape tile (150mm). Options: (1) One full 150mm row + grout + a narrow ~25mm strip — may be too thin to cut cleanly on a fluted tile; (2) A single tile cut to 177mm from the 300mm (long) dimension, installed portrait in this strip only; (3) Adjust grout spacing in this zone to absorb the difference. Discuss with tiler for best approach.
Flute alignment — CRITICAL for tiler: Stacked bond ensures the vertical grout lines are continuous, so flutes naturally align row-to-row on all full tiles. For the cut column on the right (door side): ALWAYS cut from the RIGHT edge of the tile so the left-side flute pattern is preserved identically in every row — this keeps flutes aligned vertically within the cut column. Same rule for side strips (139mm): always cut from the same edge so flutes are consistent top to bottom. The floor cut row just shortens the flutes (height trim only) — no horizontal alignment issue. If using a wet saw, cut with the glazed face up to get a clean edge on the flute ridges.
